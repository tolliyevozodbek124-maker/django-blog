Understanding Microservices Architecture

By Diyorbek Jumanov • Jan 10, 2026 • 8 min read

Microservices architecture is an approach to building software systems as a collection of small, independently deployable services.

What is Microservices?

Each service is responsible for a specific business capability and communicates with others via APIs.

Advantages

  • Scalability
  • Independent deployment
  • Technology flexibility

Challenges

Distributed systems introduce complexity such as network latency, data consistency, and monitoring.

“Microservices are not a silver bullet — they introduce trade-offs.”

Choosing microservices should be driven by business needs and system complexity.
